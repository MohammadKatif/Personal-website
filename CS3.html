Project

Overview

The smart blind stick project is a technological innovation aimed at
enhancing the independence and safety of visually impaired individuals.
By integrating various sensors, such as ultrasonic and infrared sensors,
along with advanced navigation algorithms, this smart device can detect
obstacles, drop-offs, and potential hazards in the user's path.

It provides real-time feedback through vibration alerts, sound cues,
or even smartphone notifications, enabling the user to navigate
their surroundings with greater confidence and reduced risk

Project

Details

Traditionally constructed using a metal pipe, variations of this assistive device
can also be crafted from different materials to suit individual preferences. At
its core, the device utilizes a microcontroller, often an Arduino Nano, although
other compatible models can also be used, to orchestrate its smart functions.

Central to the system's obstacle detection mechanism is the ultrasonic sensor.
Comprising two integral parts, the sensor operates by emitting ultrasonic sound
waves, typically at frequencies beyond 20kHz, from one end towards potential
obstacles. The sound waves collide with objects in the surroundings and then
rebound towards the sensor's receiving end.

By measuring the duration between transmission and reception, the
Arduino microcontroller calculates the time taken for the sound wave's
round trip. Dividing this duration by 2 provides the one-way time, which
is then multiply by the speed of sound to get the distance between the
sensor and the obstacle.

When this distance falls below a predetermined threshold, the smart
blind stick instantly alerts the user through a variety of means. While a
common choice is a buzzer integrated into the stick, alternatives such as
vibration alerts or smartphone notifications can also be implemented.

Project

Objective

Its primary objective is to develop a technologically advanced
blind stick equipped with ultrasonic sensors, capable of detecting
obstacles and providing real-time feedback to users. This aims to
significantly enhance mobility and safety, enabling seamless
navigation through various environments.

  • Enhanced Mobility: The project's core focus is on empowering
    visually impaired individuals with real-time obstacle detection
    and feedback, significantly enhancing their mobility.
  • Educational Impact: Serving as an educational tool, the
    project inspires students and budding engineers to explore
    different realms of robotics encouraging innovation.
